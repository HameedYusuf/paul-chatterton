Funded by ESRC/N8 (2015–2016), this scoping study examined how a diverse coalition — residents, local universities, businesses, government agencies, third-sector and civil-society organisations — could come together around shared challenges facing Leeds: climate change, economic stagnation, and growing inequality.

The project unfolded across three phases. First, research questions were refined, learning clusters were established, and existing models of co-production were reviewed. Second, identical workshops were convened with each learning cluster to explore the core questions in context. Third, all partners came together for a full-day reflective workshop to synthesise findings and produce a final report.

The study demonstrated how localized, inclusive decision-making and shared research can support policy innovation — and directly laid the groundwork for the establishment of the Leeds Co-Production Lab.

**Funder:** ESRC/N8 | **Duration:** July 2015–January 2016
